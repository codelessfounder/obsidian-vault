### **Benefits of Charging GST (Even Under $75k)**

1. **Claim GST Credits on Business Purchases**
You can **claim back the GST** you’ve paid on business expenses like:
    - Laptops, monitors, phones
    - Subscriptions (e.g. Figma, AWS, Adobe, Xero)
    - Office equipment
    - Travel and fuel
    - Marketing and ads
🧮 *Example*:
If you spend $11,000 including GST on a laptop, you can claim back **$1,000** in your BAS.
2. **Looks More Professional to Clients**
Some larger clients and agencies prefer or **require a tax invoice with GST**, especially if they are registered themselves (they can claim it back).
3. **Smooth Transition If You Grow**
You avoid having to register later and reissue invoices if you suddenly hit $75k+ in income.

---

### ❌ **Drawbacks of Charging GST When You Don’t Need To**

4. **Extra Admin (BAS Every Quarter)**
You must lodge a **BAS every 3 months**, even if your revenue is $0.
It’s not hard, but it’s an extra chore.
5. **May Make You Less Competitive on Price**
Your services/products are effectively **10% more expensive** to non-GST-registered clients (like individuals or small businesses under $75k).
For example, quoting $110 for a job includes $10 GST — a competitor not charging GST could quote $100 flat.
6. **Less Relevant if Most Clients Aren’t Registered**
If your customers can’t claim GST (e.g., international clients or individuals), they bear the extra cost and it doesn’t benefit them.
