## 🔧 **PRIORITY 1: Immediate Mechanical Health Check & Servicing**

### ✅ Must-Do Within the First Week:

1. **Full logbook service (if no recent records):**
    - **Engine oil + filter**
    - **Fuel filter** (critical for diesel engines)
    - **Air filter + cabin filter**
    - **Coolant flush**
    - **Brake fluid flush**
    - **Transmission fluid + filter** (automatic)
2. **Timing chain inspection**
    - Common wear item — if it's rattly on cold starts, get it checked.
3. **Check all belts and pulleys**
    - Look for cracking, squealing, or fraying.
4. **Inspect tyres, brakes, suspension**
    - Pads/discs, shock absorbers, and tyre tread — replace as needed.
5. **Scan for error codes**
    - Ask your mechanic to run an OBD2 diagnostic scan for hidden faults.

6. **Clean or replace EGR valve** (common for buildup over time)

General service

### **1. Check Boot for Water Ingress**

- Lift boot floor and inspect under the spare wheel for dampness or standing water.
- Common issue due to worn light seals or boot rubbers.

### ✅ **2. Check and Clear Cowl Drain Holes**

- Look at the base of the windscreen under the wipers.
- Clear any leaves/debris by hand to prevent water buildup entering the cabin.

### ✅ **3. Inspect Cabin Air Filter**

- Located behind the glovebox (may need to unclip a panel).
- Replace if dirty — improves airflow and HVAC performance.

### ✅ **4. Feel Front Carpets for Dampness**

- Check both driver and passenger side for damp patches (can indicate blocked A/C drains or leaks).

### ✅ **5. Inspect Boot and Door Rubber Seals**

- Run your hands along all rubber seals to check for cracks, brittleness, or loose edges.
- Prevents water leaks and wind noise.

### ✅ **6. Clean MAF Sensor**

- Optional Torx bit needed (often T20).
- Spray with MAF cleaner to improve fuel efficiency and throttle response.

### ✅ **7. Spray EGR/Intake Cleaner**

- Use diesel-safe EGR cleaner spray into the intake tract (while engine is idling — follow can instructions).
- Helps reduce carbon buildup.

### ✅ **8. Replace Engine Oil and Filter**

- Needs basic tools (socket wrench, oil filter tool, drain pan).
- Do this every 10,000–15,000 km to protect engine and turbo.

### ✅ **9. Top Up and Check Coolant**

- Only check when engine is cold.
- Top up with proper G48 or G40 coolant (never just water).

### ✅ **10. Unclog Windscreen Washer Jets & Refill Fluid**

- Use a pin to unclog nozzles.
- Refill with quality washer fluid to ensure visibility.

B180 checklist

### **Mechanical**

- [ ] **Engine starts smoothly** (no excessive cranking or rough idle)
- [ ] **No smoke** from exhaust at idle or revving (blue = oil, black = diesel overfueling)
- [ ] **No loud turbo whine**, rattling or knocking
- [ ] **Engine mounts** not worn (look for vibration at idle)
- [ ] **No fluid leaks** (look under engine bay and undercarriage)
- [ ] **Radiator coolant is clean** and at the correct level
- [ ] **Oil level and colour** (clean brown/amber is good; black/sludgy isn't)
- [ ] **Timing chain noise** (rattling on cold start = stretched chain, expensive fix)

---

### ⚙️ **Transmission & Suspension**

- [ ] **Smooth gear changes** (no jerking or hesitation)
- [ ] **No clunks or knocks** from suspension over bumps
- [ ] **No leaking transmission fluid**
- [ ] **Shocks not blown** (especially front left — a common issue)
- [ ] **No excessive bouncing** when pushing down on the car

---

### 🛞 **Wheels, Tyres & Brakes**

- [ ] **Tyre tread depth** is legal (at least 1.5mm across all tyres)
- [ ] **Even wear** on all tyres (uneven = alignment/suspension issue)
- [ ] **Brakes feel firm**, not spongy
- [ ] **No grinding or squealing** from brakes
- [ ] **Handbrake holds** car on a slope

---
