# Option 1: Mount Solitary (2 Nights)

![](https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/gps-cs-s/AHVAwerfS-d_7aYFpu017PMo8Ywy_zbth0eJj-w85JGjG2u8PwEuCpGaNShQOdrxpAr_-h-fo3776zgSwAmsCBZDZiTzKx3feRiRolxw6nR1F1r5qPwwJoleUPOMbZCVzv04pzoxWkxd=w1589-h1000-k-no)

## **📋 Quick Stats**

- **Total Distance:** ~22km (Out-and-Back)
- **Difficulty:** Grade 5 (Technical/Challenging)
- **Total Ascent:** ~1,100m+
- **Navigation:** Topographic maps/GPS essential (off-track on the plateau).
- **Access Alert:** Glenraphael Drive is closed to vehicles near Cliff Drive. We must park near **Katoomba Station** and walk/cycle the extra 2km to reach the trailhead.

---

## **🗺️ Itinerary**

### **Day 1: The Descent & The Base**

- **The Start:** we park in Katoomba and walk to the Golden Stairs trailhead.
- **The Descent:** we descend the Golden Stairs into the Jamison Valley. It is steep but well-maintained with metal ladders.
- **Mid-Day:** we follow the Federal Pass toward our first camp.
- **The Camp:** we set up at the Ruined Castle campground.
- **Sunset:** we scramble up the Ruined Castle rocks (pack-free) for 360-degree views before heading back to camp.

### **Day 2: The Traverse & Wild Camp**

- **The Climb:** we tackle the **"Korrowall Knife Edge."** This is the most technical part of the hike-a steep, unformed boulder scramble requiring hands-on movement.
- **The Plateau:** once we reach the summit, the terrain levels out into a high-altitude heathland.
- **Wild Camp:** we pitch our tent at **Chinaman's Gully**. This is a dry, rocky campsite with "infinity-edge" views over Lake Burragorang and the southern wilds.
- **Exploration:** we walk to the eastern end of the mountain (Collett’s Lookout) in the afternoon to see the full expanse of the Kedumba Valley.

### **Day 3: The Return**

- **The Descent:** we retrace our steps back down the Knife Edge. Descent requires focus to avoid loose rock.
- **The Valley:** we trek back through the Federal Pass forest.
- **The Final Push:** we ascend the Golden Stairs. This is a significant physical challenge at the end of the trip, especially with our remaining gear.
- **Finish:** we walk the final 2km back to the vehicle in Katoomba.

---

## **⚠️ Critical Notes**

- **Water Strategy:** there is **no reliable water** on the Mount Solitary plateau. We must carry all water for Days 2 and 3 (typically 4-6 liters per person) starting from the valley floor on Day 2.
- **Road Closures:** we must not attempt the full loop via Kedumba Pass or the Giant Stairway, as several connecting sections are currently closed due to landslides. We will stick to the **"Out-and-Back"** via Golden Stairs.
- **Wild Camping Rules:** no fires are permitted on the plateau; fuel stoves only. We must practice "Leave No Trace" and carry out all waste.

# Option 2: Grose Valley Immersion (1 Night)

![[image 2.png]]

## 📋 Quick Stats

- **Distance:** ~14km Return
- **Difficulty:** Grade 4 (Moderate ridge walk; very steep descent/ascent)
- **Total Ascent:** ~600m
- **Navigation:** Clear track, but steep and sustained.
- **Access:** Mount Hay Road (unsealed). Usually 2WD accessible with care, but we should check local conditions if there has been recent heavy rain.

---

## 🗺️ Itinerary

### Day 1: The Ridge to the River

- **Start:** we arrive at the Lockleys Pylon Trailhead on Mount Hay Road.
- **The Ridge:** we begin with a 3.5km walk across the heathland. This section is relatively flat and offers sweeping views of the Grose Valley and the sandstone cliffs of Mount Banks.
- **The Pylon:** we reach the summit of Lockleys Pylon for a 360-degree panorama.
- **Shortridge Pass:** we follow the track past the pylon to the edge of the escarpment. We descend the "Shortridge Pass"-a very steep, zig-zagging track that drops roughly 500m into the valley.
- **Camp:** we arrive at Acacia Flat campground. We set up camp among the rough-barked apples and stringybarks.
- **Evening:** we explore the nearby Blue Gum Forest or sit by the Grose River as the light fades from the canyon walls.

### Day 2: The Cathedral & The Climb

- **The Morning:** we take a short, pack-free walk through the Blue Gum Forest. The towering white trunks are best viewed in the soft morning light.
- **The Ascent:** we retrace our steps back up Shortridge Pass. This is a sustained physical challenge that will take us roughly 1.5 to 2 hours of uphill effort.
- **The Return:** once we are back on the plateau, we enjoy the easy walk across the ridge as we return to the car.

---

## ⚠️ Critical Planning Notes

- **Water:** there is no water on the ridge. We need to carry at least 2L each for the walk down. We can refill at the Grose River near the campsite, but we **must boil or treat** the water before drinking.
- **Camping:** Acacia Flat is a "fuel stove only" area. No wood fires are permitted.
- **Bookings:** while the campsite is free, we must book our spot in advance via the NSW National Parks website (a small booking fee applies).
- **Waste:** this is a remote area; we must carry out everything we carry in.

# Pros and cons list!

| **Feature** | **Mount Solitary (2 Nights)** | **Lockleys Pylon / Blue Gum (1 Night)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Vibe** | Rugged, exposed, rocky heights. | Massive open ridges & lush, ancient forest. |
| **Difficulty** | **Grade 5 (Hard).** Technical scrambles. | **Grade 4 (Moderate).** Steep but defined. |
| **Physical Toll** | High. Significant "hands-on" climbing. | Moderate. Big hills but no rock climbing. |
| **Water Supply** | **Zero on plateau.** Must carry 4-6L+. | River at camp. Must treat/filter. |
| **Campsite** | Wild & Dry (Chinaman's Gully). | Remote & Sheltered (Acacia Flat). |
| **Solitude** | Very high once on the mountain. | Moderate; popular with day hikers. |
| **Car Access** | **Restricted.** Park in Katoomba (2km walk). | **Dirt Road.** Mount Hay Rd (check rain). |
