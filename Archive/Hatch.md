Onboarding questions



### Project & Workflow

- **What does a typical "task" look like?** (e.g., Is it Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), fact-checking, or creative prompt engineering?)
- **How is work assigned?** Is there a queue-based system where I claim tasks, or are specific projects assigned to me weekly?
- **What is the "Gold Standard" for this project?** Every AI firm has a specific rubric for what a "perfect" response looks like—ask if you can see their style guide or evaluation framework early.

### Quality & Feedback

- **What does the feedback loop look like?** If a lead or an automated check flags a response I’ve graded, how is that communicated to me?
- **Are there specific throughput (speed) vs. quality metrics I should aim for?** AI training often balances "tokens per hour" with accuracy; knowing which one they prioritize early on is helpful.
- **What are the most common pitfalls new trainers face on this specific model?** This shows you’re focused on high-quality output from day one.

### Tools & Tech

- **What internal platform or IDE will I be using?** If it’s a proprietary tool, ask if there is any documentation or a sandbox environment you can explore before your first day.
- **Will I be working with multimodal data?** (e.g., Just text, or will I also be evaluating images, code, or video?)

### Logistics & Team

- **Who is my direct point of contact for technical blockers?** If a tool crashes or a prompt is ambiguous, is there a Slack/Discord channel for real-time support?
- **Are there opportunities to move into specialized "Expert" tracks?** (e.g., If you have a background in coding or a specific language, ask if you can eventually pivot to those more complex domains.)
