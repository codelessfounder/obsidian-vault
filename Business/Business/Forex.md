**Fundamentals:**

9. Controlling your emotions will be the hardest thing in your trading journey
10. Find ONE pair to trade ONCE per day.
    - This will development pattern recognition and mastery of that pair
11. Identify the top and/or bottom wicks on higher time frame candles (weekly, daily, 4 hourly, sometimes 1 hour)
    - Almost all time higher frame candles must have a top and bottom wick
    - If a candle is 50% formed without a bottom wick, there is an opportunity for you downwards, following the candle as it forms it’s bottom wick
    - Lower time frame (5 mins, etc) behave similarly, though initial opening prices can give false signals
12. Seek a candlestick encyclopedia to identify trade signals on lower time frames
    - Use higher times frames to determine whether the price is going up or down
    - Use lower time frames to find an entry point. This is where you can use the encyclopedia to identify a pattern that signals a good entry
13. Create wide stop losses
    - Prices often move into areas that would be considered commonplace for a stop loss in order to use liquidity to push the price in the opposite direction
14. Avoid huge trades
    - Trade small movements instead; your *take profit* are much higher.
15. Avoid Wednesdays
    - Specifically, having an open trade on market close on wednesday. Fees will be high on these trades (see JPY pairs)
16. Be wary of economic new/activity
- Having a trades open during this time increase chances of unpredictable price volatility
17. Risk 30%, max.
