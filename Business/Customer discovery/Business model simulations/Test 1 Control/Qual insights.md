**Revenue & Profitability**

- DiscoPay is operating at a **net loss**.
- The **main cost driver** is the **cash discounts** DiscoPay is covering on behalf of customers — every time a customer wins a discount, DiscoPay pays the merchant the difference.
- Despite the 3% transaction fee, it's **not enough to offset** the frequent and sizable discounts.

**Customer Outcomes**

- 27% of customers received **immediate discounts**, directly impacting DiscoPay’s margins.
- 29.3% received **store credit** via overpayment, which funds future purchases but doesn’t generate immediate profit.
- Nearly 56% of customers experienced a **“gamified” outcome**, reinforcing the thrill aspect of the payment method.

**Merchant Impact**

- Merchants are **never losing out** — they always receive the full base price ($100), regardless of outcome.
- Overpayments create **locked-in future spending** via store credit, boosting customer retention.
- Discounts are **fully funded by DiscoPay**, making them **costless for merchants** while increasing appeal for customers.

**Strategic Insights**

- **Discount frequency must be lowered** or fees increased to make the model sustainable for DiscoPay.
- **Store credit outcomes** are a **net positive**, improving merchant stickiness without upfront cost.
- A **4–5% fee** model or limiting **high-cost discount outcomes** (like 20%) could push DiscoPay into profitability without hurting customer appeal.
