5M30M=16.7%\frac{5M}{30M} = 16.7\%
- So you’re effectively selling ~17% of the company in the seed.
- That’s **reasonable founder dilution** — actually cleaner than $20M cap (25%) and not as aggressive as $40M (12.5%).

---

### **Investor Perspective**

- **Pros**:
    - $30M cap means you’re valuing yourself ambitiously but not absurdly.
    - If you have strong traction / big vision, investors will accept it.
    - Still leaves enough ownership upside for them if Series A is at $80M–$100M+.
- **Cons**:
    - If you’re pre-product or pre-revenue, some will push back: “Why $30M when you haven’t proven much yet?”
    - They may ask: “What milestones will you hit with this $5M that justify Series A at $60–$100M?”

---

### **Benchmarks (Seed Market 2025)**

- **Pre-seed**: $5M–$15M caps are still common for idea/MVP stage.
- **Seed**: $20M–$40M caps are increasingly normal if you have traction, a solid team, and a hot market.
- **Top-tier / hype companies**: $50M+ caps at seed do happen, but usually with brand-name founders or rocketship growth.

---

### **How to Frame $30M Cap**

- **Don’t apologise** — position it as fair and market-standard for your traction.
- **Point to milestones**: “This $5M will get us to [milestone], which clearly sets us up for a $100M+ priced round.”
- **Show comps**: “Companies in our space are raising at $25–40M caps for less traction — we’re right in the middle.”

---

✅ Conclusion: **$5M SAFE @ $30M cap is ambitious but not crazy.**

- If you’re pre-product → investors may push hard.
- If you have early traction, strong team, or hot market fit → very defendable.
