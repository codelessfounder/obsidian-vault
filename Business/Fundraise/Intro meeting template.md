### My hypothesis

So based on this, I came up with a hypothesis:

*“People are willing to gamble to pay for their purchases. They will wager money in the hope of receiving discounts on what they’re buying”*

### Product to test my hypothesis

okay, so now that I am totally clear on my hypothesis, I need a way to test this, so I built Disco: 

Disco is the first online Casino where play can play casino games to bet on any purchase they’re making.

Players use Disco‘s AI to scan the purchase their making. Disco will analyse the image, return what the item is, how much the item costs, and then that data will be visible at the top of their screen when they are playing - contextualising the purchase with the casino game.

### How Will we test/measure success?

Now, how will I test this? My hypothesis is unique compared to other online casinos because instea do just playing for money, users are playing for discounts on THINGS

- So we’ll be creating a flywheel of marketing videos to show people they can gamble for whatever they buy
    - meta glasses

How will I measure success? 

Right now, the metrics that matter are the ones that validate bhevioural truth - NOT scale, NOT revenue, NOT LTV/CAC yet!

We’re raising 700K to test these 4 things: 

39. Play frequency per user - Do they play more than once? - If they try it 2–3 times, it’s not novelty — it’s behaviour.
40. Purchase Context Attachment Rate - When shown the item they scanned (e.g., “Nike Dunks – $149”), do they play *more *compared to when there’s no contextualisation? This validates your core product insight: contextual gambling increases engagement.
41. Scan-to-Play Time (Friction Test) -  How fast can a user go from scanning → playing? Long friction = low intent. Short friction + strong conversion = real desire.
42. Social Lift - What % of early users share or post? This matters because your GTM *is literally powered by virality*
