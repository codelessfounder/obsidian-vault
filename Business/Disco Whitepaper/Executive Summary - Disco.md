# **Founder's Hypothesis**

From these shifts, one insight became obvious:

> *People are willing to gamble to pay for their purchases. They will wager money in the hope of receiving discounts on what they’re buying.*

Winning money back on dinner  or a pair os sneakers feels fun — losing feels like missing a bargain, not just losing cash. This emotional reframing is powerful. It expands the casino market to people who would *never* gamble traditionally because the wager is tied to something tangible they already want.

This is the core hypothesis Disco tests.

# ** The Product to test the hypothesis: Disco**

Disco is the first online casino where players wager to cover real purchases. By scanning an item with Disco’s AI image analysis feature, users turn everyday transactions into moments of chance. They play Coin Flip, Mines, or Dice not just for cash, but for sneakers, coffees, meals, travel, and whatever they’re actually buying.

## **How It Works**

1. User scans or uploads a photo of what they’re buying
2. Disco’s AI identifies the item and extracts the price
3. This purchase appears at the top of the game
4. User plays any table casino game (Coin Flip, Mines, or Dice etc)
5. Winning = Player doubles their item stake, simulating a free purchase
6. Losing = Player loses their item stake

It reframes gambling from an abstract financial activity into a contextual, personal risk-reward moment tied to real life.

This round is explicitly designed to validate behavioural truth, testing 4 key metrics:

The faster someone scans → plays, the higher the innate desire.

Virality is a core part of the GTM — this must be measured early.
