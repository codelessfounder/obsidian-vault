**Company Vision**

To create purpose-driven apps that are obsessively focused on delivering value to the customers of startup founders and business enterprises.

“*we believe in a world where businesses are in control of their technology ownership without the burdens of expensive maintenance and updates”*

**Why am I starting this company**

I’m creating the product team that I never had as a first time founder.

When I was trying to build my MVP for my first startup, I was effectively scammed out of $20,000 by a software agency in Sydney who failed to deliver a product.

I never want a founder to go through what I went through when I was building my first startup. So, I’ve spent the last couple of years learning about how effective product team build apps that customers need and love to use.

To get from idea to execution as fast as possible so as to be able to provide accessible app developments for startup founders.

| good teams of product design and engineering sit side by side and they embrace the give and take between the functionality the user experience and the enabling technology | Bad teams sit in their respective silos and ask what others make requests for the services in the form of documents and scheduled meetings |

| good teams ensure that their engineers have time to try out the prototypes in discovery every day, so that they can contribute their thoughts on how to make the product better | Bad teams show their prototypes to the engineers during sprint planning so they can estimate |
| good teams engage directly with end users and customers every week to better understand their customers and to see the customer's response to their latest ideas | Bad teams think they are the customer |
| good teams know that many of their favorite ideas won't end up working for customers and even the ones that could will need several iterations to get to the point where they provide the desired outcome | bad teams just build what's on the roadmap and are satisfied with meeting dates and ensuring quality |
