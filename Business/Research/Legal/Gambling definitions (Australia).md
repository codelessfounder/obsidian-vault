### **Table 1A: Definitions from the Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Section 4)**

| **Term** | **Definition** |
| --- | --- |
| **Gambling Service** | Encompasses various services related to betting, gaming, lotteries, and other activities commonly understood as gambling. |
| **Betting Services** | Services for placing, making, receiving, or accepting bets. |
| **Introduction Services** | Services aimed at introducing individuals who want to bet to those willing to accept bets. |
| **Lottery Services** | Services for conducting or supplying lotteries. |
| **Gaming Services** | Games played for money or value, involving chance or a combination of chance and skill, with consideration to play. |
| **Other Gambling Services** | Any other service that fits the ordinary meaning of a gambling service. |

---

### **Table 1B: Prohibited Interactive Gambling Services (Section 5)**

| **Aspect** | **Description** |
| --- | --- |
| **Prohibited Interactive Gambling Service** | A gambling service provided via the internet or other electronic means, unless specifically excluded. |
| **Key Criteria** | Provided in the course of business through internet, carriage services, broadcasting, content, or datacasting services. |
| **Exclusions** | Includes telephone betting services, excluded wagering services, and excluded gaming services (detailed further in the Act). |

---

### **Table 2: Exclusions from Prohibited Interactive Gambling Services**

| **Exclusion Category** | **Description** | **Conditions for Exclusion** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Telephone Betting Services** | Betting services conducted via telephone. | Must be genuine voice communication, not automated or internet-based calls. |
| **Excluded Wagering Services** | Includes online betting on horse racing, greyhound racing, harness racing, and sports (pre-event only). | Must be pre-event (no in-play betting) and offered by licensed Australian operators. |
| **Excluded Gaming Services** | Covers services like online lotteries where outcomes are determined by a draw. | Must not involve instant win mechanisms; operators need appropriate licensing and regulatory compliance. |
| **General Conditions** | Although excluded from being "prohibited," these services are still regulated. | Operators must comply with federal and state laws, obtain licenses, and implement consumer protections. |
| **Unlicensed Services** | Unlicensed regulated services remain prohibited, regardless of whether they fall into an excluded category. | Provision of gambling services without proper licensing is illegal under the IGA. |
