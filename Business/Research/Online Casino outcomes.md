Online casino game outcomes are determined using **pseudo-random number generators (PRNGs)**. Here's a technical breakdown:

---

### 🧠 1. **Pseudo-Random Number Generators (PRNGs)**

At the core of every game — whether it's slots, roulette, blackjack, or poker — is a **PRNG algorithm** that produces a sequence of numbers that appear random but are deterministically generated from an initial value called a **seed**.

### Example Algorithms:

- **Mersenne Twister** (commonly used for its long period and good distribution)
- **Xorshift**, **PCG**, or **Cryptographically Secure PRNGs** in high-security environments

These are not *truly* random — they're **deterministic** and can be reproduced if the seed is known.

---

### 🔐 2. **Seeding & Entropy**

- The seed value is often based on variables like system clock, mouse movements, or entropy sources from the operating system (`/dev/random`, `crypto.getRandomValues()`, etc.).
- For **provably fair games** (especially in crypto casinos), seeds come from both the **server** and the **client** to ensure neither party can manipulate the outcome unilaterally.

---

### 🎰 3. **Mapping Random Numbers to Game Outcomes**

The output of the PRNG is mapped to in-game outcomes using predefined logic:

### For example:

- **Slot machine**: Each number might correspond to a reel position. A combination of three PRNG outputs forms the final symbol outcome.
- **Roulette**: A PRNG output mod 37 gives the number (0–36).
- **Card games**: A shuffled deck is generated by mapping PRNG outputs to unique card positions.

---

### 📊 4. **Return-to-Player (RTP) & House Edge Logic**

- PRNGs generate outcomes, but game logic is designed such that **long-term probabilities** favour the house.
- For example, a slot machine might simulate 1 million spins and adjust symbol weights to maintain an **RTP of 96%**, meaning players lose 4% over time.

---

### ✅ 5. **Regulatory Testing & Certification**

- Games are audited by bodies like **eCOGRA**, **GLI**, or **iTech Labs** to ensure:
    - PRNG is statistically sound
    - No bias or manipulation exists
    - The software complies with licensed RTP and volatility targets

---

### ⚠️ 6. **Cryptographic PRNGs in Blockchain Casinos**

- In blockchain-based casinos (e.g., on-chain games), randomness might use:
    - **Chainlink VRF** (Verifiable Random Function)
    - Block hashes + player seeds
    - Commitment schemes (e.g., hash of server seed revealed post-bet)

These methods make the random outcome **auditable and tamper-proof**.
