VGW (Virtual Gaming Worlds) operates in a legal grey area by **structuring its platforms around sweepstakes laws**, which are **distinct from gambling laws** in many jurisdictions—especially in the United States. Here's how they navigate it:

---

### 🔍 The Legal Loophole: Sweepstakes vs Gambling

**Most jurisdictions define gambling** as involving **three elements**:

1. **Consideration** – the player pays or wagers money.
2. **Chance** – the outcome is based on randomness.
3. **Prize** – the player can win something of value.

**Remove any one of these**, and it’s usually **not considered gambling** under the law.

---

### ✅ VGW’s Model: How It Sidesteps Gambling Regulations

VGW platforms (like **Chumba Casino**, **LuckyLand Slots**, and **Global Poker**) use a **dual-currency sweepstakes system**:

### 1. **Gold Coins (GC)**

- **Play-money currency**, used for fun.
- Has **no cash value**, and is **freely given** or purchased in bundles (e.g., buy $20 of Gold Coins and receive free Sweeps Coins as a bonus).

### 2. **Sweeps Coins (SC)**

- A **promotional currency**, used to enter sweepstakes games.
- **Cannot be bought directly** — you receive them:
    - As a **free bonus** when purchasing Gold Coins,
    - Through **mail-in requests** (yes, real physical mail),
    - Or via **social media giveaways or promotions**.
- SC **can be redeemed for cash prizes**, which is what players really want.

### 🛡️ Key Legal Shields

- **No direct purchase of sweepstakes entries** (SC).

- **Games are technically sweepstakes promotions**, not gambling.
- **Terms and conditions** are carefully worded to avoid violating “gambling” terminology (e.g., no mention of “wagering” or “betting”).

### 📬 The Mail-In Option: Why It Matters

In the U.S., to comply with **sweepstakes law**, users **must be able to enter for free**. VGW honors this by letting players send a postcard or letter with their details to request free SC.

This “no purchase necessary” clause keeps them within the legal definition of a **sweepstakes promotion**, rather than a gambling operation.

### 🎰 Why This Works (So Far)

- **Sweepstakes laws** are regulated at the **state level** in the U.S., and are **much looser** than gambling laws.
- The **FTC and state AGs** regulate sweepstakes, but enforcement is rare as long as:
    - There’s **no deception**,
    - Players have **a fair chance to win**,
    - And there’s **a free method to enter**.
