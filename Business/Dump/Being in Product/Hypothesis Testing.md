Lazy registration: circumventing the sign-up process

Benefit:

- Users are able to use and take value from your product without and are only asked to register once they have benefitted from the product

This is essential for fast hypothesis testing:

e.g. WC would be able to more quickly test whether consumers were willing to adopt a new way of offer and acceptance of online luxury watch transactions if physical authentication was involved

Hypothesis testing may require product owners to manage more than one class of users:

1. Unregistered users
2. Registered (trial) users
3. Users who have paid the premium for the service

The more classes of users there are, the more work is required to keep track of them.

Split testing seems to be one of the best industry practices for learning about customers

The 3 A’s of metrics:

4. Actionable
- For a report to be considered actionable, it must demonstrate clear cause and effect (otherwise it is a vanity metric)
- People are better able to learn from their actions through actionable metrics
- *Human beings are innately talented learners when given a clear and objective assessment*
- **How do we determine cause and effect? Split testing?**
5. Accessible
- Related to reports
- Startups are often too focused on using learned data to get what they want rather than as genuine feedback to guide their future actions
- Make reports as simple as possible **so everyone **understands them. “Metrics are people too”
- *Cohort-based *reports are the gold standard of learning metrics: they turn complex actions into people-based reports
    - *e.g. among the people who used our product in this period, here’s how many of them exhibited behaviours we care about*
- A good practice (done at *Grockit):*
    - Daily document automatically generated containing the latest data for every split test experiment and leap of faith metric. This was mailed to everyone in the company every day.
6. Auditable:
- We must ensure that data is credible to employees
- We need to be able to test the data found in the accessible reports by hand, by talking to customers “in the messy real world”.
    - This is the only way to check if the report contains true facts
    - Managers need the ability to spot check the data with real customers
