Intro:

- Ideas are cheap; execution speed matters more.
- Great product teams center on two disciplines: discovery and delivery.
- Reducing idea-to-execution cycle time is the core operating principle.

Good vs bad product teams:

| Good teams | Bad teams |
| --- | --- |
| Mission-led with compelling product vision | Mercenary execution |
| Learn from customer struggle and new technology | Gather static requirements from sales/stakeholders |
| Prototype rapidly and validate before delivery | Prioritise roadmaps in meetings |
| Product, design, and engineering collaborate daily | Functional silos with handoffs |
| Run continuous experiments safely | Wait for permission to test |
| Engage users weekly and iterate fast | Assume they are the customer and just ship roadmap items |
| Instrument usage and release continuously | Treat analytics as optional and batch risky releases |
| Obsess over reference customers and business impact | Obsess over competitors and output volume |
