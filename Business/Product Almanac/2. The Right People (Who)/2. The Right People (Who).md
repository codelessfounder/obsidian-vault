### Consolidated notes

*Trust is a function of competence and character. Competence includes skills and track record; character includes integrity, motive, and intent.*

- Hire for competence, but hiring for potential is valid if you commit to active coaching.
- Look for teammates who are not all exactly like you.
- Run reference checks yourself and ask directly if the referee would hire the candidate again.
- Review candidate social behavior signals when relevant.
- Make the reciprocal growth agreement explicit: company invests in development, person invests in mission and outcomes.
- Recruiting is continuous: write, teach, and share product craft publicly to attract talent over time.
- Strong recruits can appear in unexpected roles; relationships often compound over months or years.
- Give value first (books, articles, insights) and plant long-term seeds with high-potential people.

Interviewing:

- In early startups, the CEO/product lead owns interview quality and candidate experience.
- Interview panels should demonstrate both competence and character.
- Hiring high-potential "Scarletts" is viable only when the team commits to active coaching.
- If competence does not emerge in reasonable time, leadership should own and correct the hiring miss.
- Interview prompt: ask candidates to rank execution, creativity, strategy, and growth from strongest to weakest.

Remote employees:

- Discovery and delivery both matter; remote teams often do delivery well but struggle on discovery.
- Avoid artifact-heavy handoffs; center collaboration on shared problem solving and prototypes.
- Protect psychological safety and coach remote communication norms.
