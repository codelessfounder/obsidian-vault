The expectation of product managers is that they work in the realm of 60hrs a week to be effective in their role.

A product team is comprised of the following:

1. A product manager
2. 2-10 engineers
3. A product designer (if the product is user facing)

In short, this is the role of the product manager:

*“The product manager is responsible for evaluating opportunities and determining what gets built & delivered to customers.”*

The hardest part about this is actually determining what goes into the product backlog is actually worth building for your users.

Engineers and designers WILL ask for evidence to show what you’re asking them to build is truly worth building. If they don’t, show them anyway.

“*When the product succeeds, its because everyone did what they needed to do. When it fails, it is the product manager’s fault.”*

A product manager must:

- Have a sophisticated understanding of modern technologies and trends
- Have a deep understanding of the business
- Have a deep understanding of data analytics
- Have a deep understanding of customer behaviour
- Have a deep understanding of competitors
- Have credibility among the key executives
- Passion for the product

Product managers will almost always start their day with half an hour looking at analytics tools, understanding what has been happening in the past 24hrs. This is usually

- sales and usage analytics
- A/B test results

Product managers should undertake 2 academic courses to excel in their role:

4. Introduction to computer programming course
- This will expand your technological horizons and give you a better appreciation for the work of the engineers in your team and the tech that is used to enable solutions for your customers
5. Introduction to business and accounting finance
- The will allow you to have a deep understanding of business and customers

A product manager’s relationship with engineers:

There are typically two conversation you are having with your engineers:

6. You are soliciting their ideas and inputs for items in product discovery
7. They are asking you clarifying questions on the items they’re working on delivering to production

The morale of engineers is very much the function and responsibility of the product manager.

You should be giving engineers as much latitude as possible so they have the opportunity to come up with the best solutions.

Involving engineers deeply in exposure to customer pain will aid in making them feel like missionaries.

Do not shelter them. They need a visceral understanding of customer problems.
