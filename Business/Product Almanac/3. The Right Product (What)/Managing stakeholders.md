*“You must be willing to disrupt your own organisation, or someone else will”*

A stakeholder is someone who has veto power; they prevent your work from launching.

Every time you build something that goes against the company stakeholders, a little bit of trust is lost in you and your product team. The company loses trust in you.

The product manager must:

1. Convince each stakeholder that they understand the issues and product
2. Commit to coming up with solutions that work for customers AND the stakeholders

Strategies to achieve this:

- Stakeholders want to be informed around important decisions are changes
- They must give you room to come up with the best solutions possible, even if they solutions varies substantially from the solution they envisioned
- First and foremost, you must be a competent product manager
- Openly and regularly share learnings
- Sit with each stakeholder and understand their constraints; this will improve your solutions
- Preview solutions during discovery with the stakeholders individually
- Run a test, collect evidence and spend 30 mins with each stakeholder to keep them in the loop and get feedback
- A weekly lunch of coffee with stakeholders works well
- PMs should not trust a sign off from stakeholders on anything less than a high fidelity prototype
    - This creates transparency, measures expectations and reduces the risk of of shock, frustration and anger if the solution is substantially different from what they envisioned
- DO NOT discuss with all stakeholders in one room
- Be sensitive to the fact that some stakeholders may not understand what you do. You may have to spend some time explaining what you do.

*have a very strong and very intentional product culture that's very well established so that new hires know they're joining a different company; a different type of company that takes pride in how they work and using best practices.*

- *Make this explicit in the interview and the onboarding process*
