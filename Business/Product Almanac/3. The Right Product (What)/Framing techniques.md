Framing techniques are employed before discovery. There are two goals of framing:

1. To ensure the team is aligned in their purpose
- What business objectives we’re solving
- Which customers we’re solving the problem for
- How we’ll know if we’ll succeed
2. Identify the big risks that need to be tackled during discovery work
- Value, usability, feasibility etc

“*Founders will stew on a solution for months if not years before they get the funding and nerve to pursue it. This is an egregious mistake. You must fall in love with the problem, not the solution. More often than not, initial solutions don’t solve the problem in a way that can power a successful business”*

**Framing technique: Opportunity Assessment **

A simple but effective technique that seeks to answer 4 basic, but extremely important questions:

3. What is the business objective?
- What is the work intending to address?
4. How will you know if you have succeeded?
- This is identifying key metrics
5. What problem will this solve for customers?
- Customer problem
6. What type of consumer are we focused on?
- Target market

The outcome is deeply understanding who the primary intended beneficiary of the product is. Everyone on the product team must know the answer to each of these four questions before you jump into product discovery work.

**Framing technique: Customer Letter **

An imaginary customer feedback statement written by the product manager to express why they are so happy with the new product and its benefits. It is a great evangelism tool, creating empathy around the customer’s current pain and more clearly shows the team how their efforts are making a visceral impact on people’s lives. It keeps the team focused on outcome, not output.

**Framing technique: Startup Canvas **

When you are inventing an entirely new product, this technique allows you to validate your value proposition, figuring out how you intend to make money, how you plan to get this product out to your customers, and to sell to them and how much it will cost to produce and sell this product.
