*“Customers don’t have to buy products and users don’t have to choose a feature… They do so when they perceive real value; the customer must understand your product to be better than what they are currently using.”*

- Quantitative learning is for proving something
- Qualitative learning is rapid learning and big insights

The purpose of Qualitative value testing is to figure out why customer are not responding to the product in the way we hoped they would

==It’s really a game of pattern recognition, where you can figure out where your product falls short once you’ve got enough similar responses from customers.==

*Qualitative value testing is arguably the most important discovery activity for you and your product team.*

2-3 tests like this should be conducted every week

==These test can only be conducted when the user has an understanding of what your product is and how it works - only then can we have a conversation about value or lack thereof.==

Have an engineer present

Use high fidelity prototypes

Use money to demonstrate value - we want customers to be ready to pull out their credit card, but not actually take their money.

Use reputation - how likely would they be to recommend this to their friends?

Time to demonstrate value - if a person is willing to schedule significant amounts of time to work on this, it shows value.

Ask users for their credentials for the platform they will be switching from to use your platform.
