A reference customer is a real customer; someone who has used your product in production, not a trial or a prototype and who has paid real money for the product. They are willing to tell other about the product and tell people voluntarily and sincerly.

These reference customers could be the best leading indicator of future product success

There are 4 variations of this technique:

1. Building Products for businesses
2. building Platform products
3. building customer enabling tools used by employees of your company
4. building products for customers

You need 6 reference customers.

Reference customer Litmus test?

Try not to launch a product in the market place before you have these 6 customers. Find 6 reference customers, THEN turn on sales and marketing.

These reference customers should reflect a specific target market; it will help sales go after those specific customers.

Reference customers need to be willing to spend time testing out early prototypes and helping the team ensure the product works well for them.

They act as partners in coming up with this product, they are not having a custom solution built specifically for them.

These reference customers are hungry and desperate for a solution.

“*If you’re having trouble finding four or five prospective customers for this effeort, it is very possible the problem you’re chasing isn’t that important.”*

Release the product to these people before anyone else. When you launch, they will stand up for you.

NB: For consumer prodcuts, you need in the realm of 10-50 reference customers

once we have those six reference customers, we can aggressively and effectively sell that product so to other customers in that market. So each reference customer is a truly significant milestone.
