1. **Planning technique: Story Map **

Horizontal axis: Major user activities

- Organised from left to right in the order you woud do them

Vertical axis: Progression level of detail

2. **Planning technique: Customer Interviews**

Interview outcomes:

*Do customers really have the problem you think they have?*

*How does the customer solve this problem today?*

*What would be required for them to switch to a new product?*

There should be a regular cadence of CI - two to three hours a week.

You must frame customer interviews as a source of learning and understanding, not trying to prove anything.

You need to be talking to people in your intended target market

You need to be meeting them in person, observing them in their own environment

3. **Planning technique: Hack days**

A quick way to get a range of high potential ideas that are focused on solving real business problems.

Two types:

4. Directed
- There is a specific business objective to be addressed for a specific customer problem. Product teams self organise to work on ideas to address this objective
5. Undirected
- Teams are able to explore any product idea they see fit so long as its related the to company mission and business problem they’re solving

There is a massive cultural benefit of regularly engaging in hack days where the engineers are involved in the ideation stage. Relationships between team members grow.


6. **Planning technique: Pilot Team **

A new team within the organisation that allows you to compare their ability to deliver business on business outcomes. These people are co-located and the team is largely in control of how they work and not dependent on teams that work in the old way.
