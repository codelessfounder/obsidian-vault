##  Deal timeline:

- Outreach 
- Initial conversation: 10-15 mins 
- Follow up/deep dive/Investory profiling 
- VC diligence 
- Term sheet 



### 1. The First Conversation (Discovery)

**Goal:** Spark interest, not close the deal.

**Timeframe:** Day 0

The initial meeting is about clarity, conviction, and narrative. You want the investor to leave thinking: “This founder is onto something — I want to learn more.”

You don’t need commitment at this point; you need curiosity. A strong first meeting will usually lead to a request for your deck or a follow-up conversation.

### 2. Follow-Up (Deck and Product)

**Goal:** Provide materials for internal sharing.

**Timeframe:** Within 24 hours

Send:

- A concise 10–12 slide deck that mirrors your spoken story.
- A product demo link or short Loom walkthrough.
- A short, tailored note reinforcing one key point from the meeting.

This helps the investor share your company internally with minimal friction.

### 3. Second Meeting (Deep Dive)

**Goal:** Demonstrate depth of thinking and awareness of key levers.

**Timeframe:** 3–7 days after the first call

Expect detailed questions about:

- Go-to-market plans
- Early traction or validation
- Revenue models and growth hypotheses

You are not expected to have every answer, but you should show structured thinking and intellectual honesty. This stage determines whether they will champion you internally.

### 4. Partner or Investment Committee (IC) Meeting

**Goal:** Secure internal approval.

**Timeframe:** 1–3 weeks after initial contact

Your main contact (the “sponsor”) now presents your case to the rest of the partnership.

You may or may not be invited to this session, depending on the firm’s culture.

The clarity and memorability of your narrative become critical here, as your sponsor is now selling *you* to others.

### 5. Diligence

**Goal:** Confirm there are no red flags.

**Timeframe:** Several days to two weeks

Pre-seed diligence is typically light and focused on:

- Cap table and ownership structure
- IP ownership and company formation documents
- Basic product and user validation
- Informal reference checks

They’re ensuring you’re credible and organised, not conducting deep financial analysis.

### 6. Term Sheet

**Goal:** Receive and agree to investment terms.

**Timeframe:** Around 3–5 weeks from the first meeting

If the firm is serious, you’ll receive a non-binding term sheet outlining:

- Valuation or SAFE cap
- Investment amount
- Ownership percentage or discount
- Key rights (pro-rata, board rights, information rights)

This is where negotiation happens, ideally supported by another interested party or your counsel.

### 7. Legal Documents and Closing

**Goal:** Execute agreements and prepare for funds transfer.

**Timeframe:** 1–2 weeks

Depending on the instrument:

- **SAFE** rounds close quickly (a few pages via DocuSign).
- **Equity** rounds require longer legal review and shareholding agreements.

During this stage, coordinate with your lawyer and provide company banking details for the wire transfer.

### 8. Funds Wired

**Goal:** Final transfer of investment capital.

**Timeframe:** Typically 3–10 business days after signing

You’ll receive a wire confirmation, updated cap table, and any onboarding communications from the investor. The round is then officially closed.

### Typical Timeline Summary

| Stage | Duration | Cumulative Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| First conversation | Day 0 | Day 0 |
| Follow-up | +1 day | 1 day |
| Deep dive | +3–7 days | 1 week |
| Partner/IC | +1–3 weeks | 2–3 weeks |
| Diligence | +1 week | 3–4 weeks |
| Term sheet | +1 week | 4–5 weeks |
| Closing & wire | +1–2 weeks | 5–7 weeks |

A normal pre-seed fundraising process takes approximately 5–7 weeks from first conversation to money in the bank. Competitive rounds can close in 10–14 days if investor interest is high and documentation is straightforward.

### Power Dynamics Over Time

| Stage | Who Leads | Key Dynamic |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Early (Discovery) | Founder | Founder drives narrative and tone |
| Middle (Diligence & Term Sheet) | Investor | Investor controls process and pacing |
| End (Closing) | Founder | Founder decides whom to accept and final terms |

Understanding where you are in this arc allows you to adjust your tone and behaviour — from confident storyteller, to pragmatic operator, to decisive closer.
